Cold water first:
  • Not a breakthrough, and not “cosmogenesis truth found”

1. What are we trying to do?

Standard cosmology is excellent at: what the sky looks like (fitting data).

QNM asks one more step: can those readouts be traced from a disclosed generative rulebook, step by step, in a way outsiders can audit and try to falsify — rather than tune after the fact?

So we compare who handed in homework on which step, not who has more citations.

2. Why one shared ruler?

Private scoreboards make every team “first.” We published a shared checklist and score our track and peers (no-boundary, GFT, causal sets, …) with the same questions. Only then is an “advantage” meaningful — and criticisable.

3. Three hard gates (like building a house)

① Dynamics — still open Own: FAIL
Most peers: FAIL

Is there a real generative dynamics that does not smuggle the answer?

② Record — we passed Own: PASS (Soft Hold B1)
Most peers: FAIL

Is there a checkable classical record / pointer structure — not just the slogan “it classicalises”?

③ Branch choice — still open Own: FAIL
Most peers: FAIL

Why our branch among many histories? Selection must weld to paid dynamics.

All three are needed before anyone may claim cosmogenesis closure. Today: 1 pass, 2 fail → written as 1/3. Listed peers are often 0/3 on the same ruler.

What the advantage actually is

Not “we are closer to the whole truth,” but: on leaving a checkable record, we filed public, killable, comparable intermediate work; many famous narratives still cannot pass that strict question.

4. What is Soft Hold?

Roughly: a soft stage + record rule — the theory must support a readable record structure, not only abstract equations. B1 is one sealed homework block (acceptance bars + kill controls). Passing it pays the record gate; it does not finish the building.

5. Do peers have nothing?

No. On other rulers:

  • GFT-like programmes often have denser pregeometry/dynamics shape — we still learn from that slice.
  • No-boundary / tunneling often have stronger selection-shape language — yet still often fail record true-payment.
  • ΛCDM wins observation — and is not a “laws-from-where” track.

A: No. Comparison is not denigration.

A: The two still-open gates: true dynamics, and selection welded to paid dynamics.
